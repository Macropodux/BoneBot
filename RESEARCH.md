## 1. Disease burden — who, and how many

- **~200 million women worldwide** have osteoporosis (femoral-neck T-score <= -2.5).
  Prevalence rises with age: ~10% at 60 -> ~20% at 70 -> ~40% at 80 -> two-thirds by 90.
  Under WHO criteria, **21.2% of women over 50** are affected (vs 6.3% of men).
  - *Denominator matters:* ~200M = women only, strict definition. The broader
    "~500M people" figure counts **both sexes 50+** and/or includes low bone
    mass/osteopenia. GBD 2021 re-labels the same ~200M as "low bone mineral
    density." **Never conflate 200M and 500M — always state the definition.**
- **United States:** 54 million adults 50+ have osteoporosis **or** low bone mass;
  of the **10.2 million with osteoporosis, >80% are women** (~8M). (NHANES
  2005-2010 / Wright 2014.)
- **Europe** (EU27 + UK + Switzerland, 2019): 32 million with osteoporosis, of whom
  **25.5 million are women** = 22.1% of all women aged 50+. (SCOPE 2021, Kanis et al.)

### Lifetime risk — the emotional hook
- **~1 in 3 women over 50 will suffer a fragility fracture** in her remaining
  lifetime (vs ~1 in 5 men). This is **conservative** — mortality-adjusted data
  (Nguyen 2007) gives 44%, and UK ROS data reports up to **1 in 2**.
- Women''s lifetime risk of *any* fracture is **40-50%** (vs 13-22% for men).
- **A woman''s lifetime hip-fracture risk (~1 in 6) exceeds her breast-cancer risk
  (~1 in 9).** (Cummings et al. 1989; holds against modern ~1-in-8 estimates.)
  This single line lands in a room.

## 2. The screening gap — THE wedge (the demo hook)

The strongest, best-evidenced part of the whole case: **the disease is common,
under-screened, AND cheap to flag.**

- **Only 12.3% of US Medicare women (65+) had a bone-density test *before* their
  index fragility fracture.** (Keshishian 2017, n=103,852.)
  *2-1 vote — cohort is the older Medicare subset; "BMD test" slightly glosses
  "DXA". Direction is robust; quote with that scope.*
- **In an 8-country European primary-care study of 3,798 women aged 70+, fewer than
  1 in 3 had ever had a DXA scan** (24.9% overall; only Belgium, Switzerland,
  Ireland reached 37-45%). (McCloskey 2022.) This is a GP-attending high-risk
  sample, so it *understates* the general-population gap.
- **Under-diagnosis:** fewer than **50% of at-risk women** carry an osteoporosis
  diagnosis (20.3% Germany -> 43.2% Slovakia); **up to 80%** of patients who
  fracture remain undiagnosed/untreated; only ~1/3 of vertebral fractures ever come
  to clinical attention.

### UK / NHS access — gold for a London pitch
- The **UK ranks 23rd of 29** European countries for DXA scanners per capita.
- **89% of NHS Trusts** have fewer than 1 DXA scanner per 100,000 (the
  international standard); **71%** have fewer than 0.5.
- Patients waiting **>6 weeks for a DXA rose ~9x (2,245 -> ~20,000) from 2020-2022**;
  46% of services missed the 6-week target, 26% exceeded 13 weeks.
- *Source: Royal Osteoporosis Society (Jan 2024), from an APPG inquiry + FOI to 160
  NHS Trusts; corroborated by the Society of Radiographers. Advocacy framing, but
  FOI-derived. ~2.5 yrs old; 2020 baseline may be COVID-depressed (inflating "9x").*

Framing: the scanner pathway can''t cope; a free triage tool sends only the
*right* women to a scarce scan.

### The downstream treatment gap (supporting, not primary)
- Europe (EU27+2) had a **71% treatment gap** in 2019 — **~14.0 million women**
  eligible for therapy left untreated (prefer the peer-reviewed 14.0M over IOF''s
  rounded 15M). Rose from 55% (2010) -> 71% (2019).
- Two-thirds of UK osteoporosis patients receive no treatment. In the US,
  post-hip-fracture medication use fell from >40% (2002) to ~20.5% (2011);
  72.2% of Medicare women got no osteoporosis medication within 12 months of a
  fragility fracture.

## 3. Economic burden — why a payer cares

- **EU6** (FR, DE, IT, ES, UK, SE) fragility-fracture cost: **EUR 29.6B (2010) ->
  EUR 37.5B (2017, +27%) -> projected EUR 47.4B by 2030.** (Borgstrom et al. 2020.)
- **~USD 43,669** total 12-month cost per hip-fracture patient (global pooled
  meta-regression, Williamson 2017; inpatient care is the main driver).
- **Do not combine** the EUR-aggregate (EU6, incl. indirect costs) with the
  USD-per-patient figure — different studies, scopes, years. USD 43,669 is a global
  pooled estimate, not US- or UK-specific.

## Honesty landmines (read before you present)

1. **"1 in 3 women" is lifetime FRACTURE risk — NOT prevalence.** The claim that
   1-in-3 women over 50 *have* osteoporosis was **refuted 0-3**. Never say "1 in 3
   women have osteoporosis." Say "**1 in 3 will fracture**."
2. **State the definition** on every global number: 200M (women, strict, T <= -2.5)
   vs 500M (both sexes 50+ / incl. low bone mass).
3. **Source vintage:** US prevalence = NHANES 2005-2010; US screening/treatment =
   2011; UK DXA access = Jan 2024. All are the correctly-attributed authoritative
   standards, but none are "current-year."
4. **Diagnosis != closes the treatment gap.** The claim that getting women diagnosed
   substantially shrinks the treatment gap was **refuted (1-2)** — don''t use it.

## Refuted / excluded (kept for transparency)

- *"Osteoporosis affects 1 in 3 women over 50 worldwide (prevalence)"* — **0-3.**
  The real 1-in-3 statistic is lifetime *fracture* risk. Do not restate as prevalence.
- *"The treatment gap shrinks substantially once a diagnosis exists (36.5-79.4%
  reduction)"* — **1-2.** Not supported strongly enough to use.

## Gaps & open questions (the next research pass, before the venture case relies on it)

1. **Market size** — real size + CAGR of femtech / women''s-health-tech / digital
   health / menopause tech / bone densitometry, and a defensible TAM/SAM for a
   digital screening-and-triage tool. *No verified figures yet.*
2. **Go-to-market comparables** — how femtech / menopause / bone-health startups
   acquire users and monetize (payers, employer/menopause benefits, HRT clinics,
   GP triage, pharmacy, wearable integrations, content/SEO, communities); named
   direct comparables. *Unresearched.*
3. **Regulatory classification** — is a "screening flag, not a diagnosis" tool a
   general-wellness product or Software as a Medical Device under US FDA / UK MHRA
   / EU MDR? Have any comparable non-imaging bone-risk tools been cleared?
   *Unresearched.*
4. **No-imaging prediction validity** — published accuracy of predicting DXA
   T-score risk *without* imaging (NHANES-style models on profile + activity +
   labs) vs FRAX / OST. **This underpins our whole "validated model" credibility
   claim and was outside the verified set — research it before leaning on it.**
