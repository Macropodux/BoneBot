# Challenge 05 Brief — Foundation Models for Women's Hormonal Health

This source document is the official prompt and guidelines for Challenge 05, "Foundation Models for Women's Hormonal Health," presented at the Hack-Nation 6th Global AI Hackathon in collaboration with the MIT Clubs of Northern California and Germany.

## Goals and Motivation

Female physiology is historically underrepresented in biomedical and AI research. Menstrual, metabolic, and menopause-related health changes are continuous and dynamic, yet healthcare options are typically static. The challenge encourages participants to build open AI infrastructure (dataset, benchmark, model, or application) to close the women's health gap, which could unlock $1 trillion in annual global economic impact.

## The Challenge Directive

Teams are asked to contribute a single, reusable building block to the scientific community rather than attempting a massive standalone foundation model:
1. **Data & Benchmark Infrastructure:** Curate, integrate, and publish datasets combining wearable, laboratory, imaging, and symptom data. Focus on train/val/test splits, evaluation scripts, and target prediction tasks (ovulation, hormone levels, menopause stages, disease-risk).
2. **AI Model Infrastructure:** Train a focused, reproducible, and explainable model (e.g. menopause onset signatures, clinical trial safety, explainable multi-source integrations).
3. **Application Infrastructure:** Build consumer applications (voice-based logging, digital twins, symptom tracking) grounded on reusable datasets or models.

## Key Deliverables & Rules

- **Open Science Requirement:** Publish all datasets, benchmarks, model checkpoints, source code, and evaluation pipelines under an open-source license. The product must serve as a reusable scientific asset, not just an isolated prototype app.
- **Required Submissions:**
  - Working prototype and source code.
  - Technical documentation and dataset/benchmark descriptions.
  - Benchmark evaluation methodology.
  - A 3-minute demonstration video.
- **Core Datasets Recommended:**
  - **NHANES (CDC):** Demographic, reproductive health, lab (thyroid, biochemistry), and physical activity accelerometer data.
  - **mcPHASES (PhysioNet):** Menstrual cycle symptoms, Fitbit, CGM, and hormone records.
- **Success Criteria:**
  - **Women's Health Impact:** Reach and improvement in quality of life.
  - **Technical Excellence:** Rigorous, reproducible, and scalable methods.
  - **Foundation Value:** Reusable infrastructure left behind for subsequent research.
